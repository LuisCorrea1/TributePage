Sergio "Checo" Pérez

Biography

Sergio Pérez Mendoza, nicknamed Checo (born January 26, 1990 in Guadalajara, México), is currently a driver for Oracle Red Bull Racing alongside Max Verstappen. Pérez was one of four drivers who débuted in 2011 for Sauber, with the others being Pastor Maldonado, Paul di Resta and Jérôme d'Ambrosio. He is the first Mexican driver in Formula One in 30 years since Héctor Rebaque in 1981. He scored his first podium at the 2012 Malaysian Grand Prix, being the first Mexican to do so in more than 40 years. He scored his first pole position in the 2022 Saudi Arabian Grand Prix in surprise fashion narrowly beating Charles Leclerc. His first win came at the 2020 Sakhir Grand Prix, where he made one of his best comebacks comming from the bottom of the grid.

Timeline

On 4 October 2010, Sauber announced that Pérez would join the team in 2011, replacing Nick Heidfeld. He became the fifth Mexican to compete in Formula One, and the first since Héctor Rebaque competed between 1977 and 1981. He also became a member of the Ferrari Driver Academy scheme in October 2010. By the end of his contract with Sauber, he scored a total of 80 points, 14 in 2011 and 66 in 2012.

On 28 September 2012, Lewis Hamilton's decision to leave McLaren for Mercedes in 2013 was announced, and Pérez was subsequently confirmed as Hamilton's replacement. This also ended Pérez' association with Ferrari, as he was released from its driver academy. Pérez confirmed on 13 November 2013 that he would be leaving McLaren at the end of the season to be replaced by Kevin Magnussen. On 12 December 2013 (exactly a month after it was announced he would leave McLaren), Force India confirmed that Pérez would join Nico Hülkenberg in their driver line-up for 2014 in a 15 million Euro deal. He ended up scoring a total of 49 points.

On 12 December 2013, Pérez had his drive for Force India in 2014 confirmed. With a total of 7 podiums and 577 points achieved duryng his stay in Force India (later named Racing Point), Pérez became one of the most experienced drivers on the grid wich would later lead him to win his first race at the Sakhir Grand Prix in 2020.
The most major controversy of the year, however, was in Perez finding himself ousted from Racing Point, despite being under contract to the team for two more years, for Sebastian Vettel. Perez went on to score his best results of the 2020 season, while in the unusual situation of having no confirmed spot on the grid for 2021...

Pérez raced for Red Bull Racing in 2021, replacing Alex Albon - who was demoted to the role of reserve and test driver - and partnering Max Verstappen. The 2021 season turned out to be his best season in Formula 1, scoring 190 points and 5 podium finishes including a win (his first with RedBull) at the Azerbaijan Grand Prix.
His solid performance led him to sign another year with RedBull. Midway through the 2022 season, Perez has scored 173 points including 6 podium finishes along with a win at the Monaco Grand Prix. This led to another contract extension that will keep him at RedBull until 2024.
